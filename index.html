Capybara

Capybaras are of the same order as rats (Rodents) but can weight up to 150 lbs!

  • Scientific Name: Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris
  • Average Length: 3.5 to 4.4 ft
  • Average Lifespan: 8 to 10 years
  • Habitat: found throughout almost all countries of South America except Chile near bodies of water

The capybara is the largest living rodent in the world. Its close relatives include guinea pigs. It is a highly social species and can be found in groups as large as 100 individuals, but usually lives in groups of 10 to 20 individuals.
